01
✓
Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Pat the chicken completely dry with paper towels — this is the secret to crispy skin.
Grandma always said: dry bird, crispy skin. No shortcuts here.
02
✓
Rub the chicken all over with olive oil, then season generously with salt and pepper, including inside the cavity.
03
✓
Stuff the cavity with the lemon halves, garlic head, thyme, and rosemary. Tie legs together with kitchen twine if desired.
04
✓
Roast breast-side up for 60–70 minutes, until juices run clear and a thermometer reads 165°F at the thigh.
Don't open the oven in the first 45 minutes — you'll lose the heat that crisps the skin.
05
✓
Rest the chicken for 10–15 minutes before carving. Deglaze the pan with stock, scraping up the browned bits for a simple pan sauce.
Family Notes
★ Add a handful of cherry tomatoes to the roasting pan — they collapse into the sauce and it's wonderful.
★ Grandma would baste every 20 minutes. We've stopped doing that and honestly can't tell the difference.
★ Leftovers make the best chicken salad the next day.
